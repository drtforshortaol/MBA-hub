Welcome to the Open Sea

Unlike the coastal and seabed habitats, the open sea isn't tied to a touchable geological surface. It's the largest ecosystem on Earth — vast and variable, encompassing everything pelagic: the open ocean waters away from the coastal limits and above the seafloor.

Where habitats like rocky shores, kelp forest, or the seabed are defined by tangible features, the open sea is defined by intangible variables — conditions you can't touch but that shape everything that lives there.
